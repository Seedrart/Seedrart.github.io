Sze Oi 思愛

Sze Oi was born in Hong Kong. She studied charcoal and oil painting from her father. In 1992, she received her Bachelor of Fine Arts in Printmaking from Florida International University. Between 1993 and 1996, she studied Museum Education and Art Therapy under full scholarship at the Northern Illinois University. During this period, she painted with water color and received “The Best New Artist Award” at the DeKalb Art Festival.
In 1996, Sze Oi moved to Richmond, British Columbia to establish her private practice in Art Psychotherapy. She received her doctoral degree in 2004. Sze Oi was a finalist for “Richmond Arts Awards” from 2011 to 2013. She is semi-retired after 25 years as a therapist; and now is pursuing again her love for art and endeavours to portray the essence of beauty from the inside out.

Portraits by Fu Wah Lau and Sze Oi Lau

Art of Portrait

My earliest childhood memories of art are of watching my father (Fu Wah Lau) draw. I cannot forget the shadow of his back as he concentrated on drawing during the middle of many nights. He always told me, “the most important thing in a portrait is the spirit of the person. You have to observe carefully. There are different characteristics in every human being.” The portrait has to capture his/her spirit of Shen ( 神 ). In Chinese characters, the symbol of Shen is interpreted as “God”: “Shen indicates thinking, consciousness, insight and memory, and all five mental-spiritual aspects of being human, all of which depend on the heart.” (Maciocia, 1994, p. 200).

Return to Nature 

Sze Oi’s printmakings demonstrate her struggle with the forces of Modernity. She feels Modernity has produced similar struggles throughout the world: Skepticism, fragmentation, determinism, domination, relativism and conflict. Additionally, she says, “The paradox of Modernity is that it has liberated mankind from traditional authorities. The resolution of the paradox is to learn to critically negotiate meaning, purpose, and value within human nature.”

Tao of Self Meaning

Although much of Sze-oi’s art is influence by nature, she comments: ‘The Tao is a way of seeing nature: it absorbs the spirit of residing in each and every form or essence experienced; it absorbs the various changing aspects of the scene – the wind that is moving through it, the music that is in the air, and the light that is forever changing….The practice of seeing and relating to nature, and then expressing both in painting, foster spontaneity and ‘pure experience’ in an artist. The artist learns to perceive with the desirelessness attitude, and to let the essence of existence, God or Tao, reveal itself.

Emotions

Art therapy bridges our inside and outside worlds, our fantasy and our reality. Here, the most important issue is our “honesty.” From honesty, springs forth love, so that we can heal. The therapeutic process, like most creative processes, is messy, confusing and even ugly. Only with an aesthetic sense can one see beauty in ugliness. Only with a heart of compassion can one touch human dignity and integrity. Only with a mind of hope and trust can one turn fantasy into reality. Only with an empty spirit can one create something from nothing and embrace paradox.

Human Essence

Sze Oi is picking up her drawing tools again after 25 years art psychotherapy practice. She joins a quilting club and practicing life drawing. She suggests that these two medias fit perfectly to express her work. She has a gift to recognize human inner emotions, it is analogy as a naked model in front of her. She tries her best to portray the beauty essence of human being and reveal it from inside out.  Life gives scrapes. There are tastes of sweet, sour, bitter, hot.... She puts them together in a beautiful quilt.
